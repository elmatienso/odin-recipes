Mazamorra Chiquita

Description

The Mazamorra Chiquita (or Small Mazamorra if you want) is a traditional soup from Boyoc√°, a region from Colombia. This region is known because it was the scenario of most of the colombian independence

It's well known due to its variety of ingredients, among them we have spices, turbs like potatoes and vegetables between others.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of callo or menudo(these are a type of meat)
  • Juice from 1 lemon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 pound stew beef, cut into small pieces
  • 3 long onions, finely chopped
  • 1 white onion, finely chopped
  • 6 small yellow potatoes or creole potato
  • 2 large red potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup corn cooked corn
  • 1 cup canary beans, cooked
  • 1/2 cup of broad beans
  • 1 bunch of chard leaves cut into small pieces
  • Salt and pepper
  • Water

Preparation

  1. Wash the corns with warm water and rub with lemon juice. In a large pot, add the corn, baking soda, and enough water to cover.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to simmer and cook until the callus is very tender, about 1 1/2 to 2 hours or 45 minutes if using a pressure cooker. Drain the corn and discard the water.
  3. Cut the corn into small pieces and place it in a large pot and cover it with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low.
  4. Add the beef, long onion, onion, corn, and beans. Cover partially and cook for about 45 minutes. Add the potatoes and chard and cook for an additional 45 minutes or until the callus is very tender. Season with salt and pepper and serve.
